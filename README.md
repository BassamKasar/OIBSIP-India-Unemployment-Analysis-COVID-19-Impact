## Unemployment Analysis with Python

### Project Overview
Unemployment is a key economic indicator, measured as the percentage of the total labor force that is without work. This project focuses on analyzing the sharp increase in the unemployment rate in India during the **Covid-19 pandemic**.

Using data science techniques, I explored the regional and sectoral impacts of the lockdown on the Indian job market.

### Key Objectives
*   Identify the trend of unemployment rates from 2019 to 2020.
*   Compare the impact between **Urban** and **Rural** areas.
*   Analyze the state-wise (Region) distribution of unemployment.
*   Visualize the correlation between unemployment and labor participation rates.

### Tech Stack
*   **Language:** Python
*   **Libraries:** Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn
*   **Tools:** Jupyter Notebook

### Dataset Information
The analysis is based on the dataset `Unemployment in India.csv`, which contains:
- **Region:** The state in India.
- **Date:** The month of the recorded data.
- **Frequency:** Frequency of measurement (Monthly).
- **Estimated Unemployment Rate (%):** Percentage of unemployed people.
- **Estimated Employed:** Total number of employed individuals.
- **Estimated Labour Participation Rate (%):** Percentage of the population interested in working.

### Visualizations & Insights
*   **The Spike:** A massive surge in unemployment was observed across all states starting in April 2020.
*   **Urban vs Rural:** Urban areas generally maintained a higher unemployment rate compared to rural regions during the lockdown period.
*   **Hardest Hit States:** States like Tripura, Haryana, and Jharkhand showed the highest average unemployment rates during the recorded period.
